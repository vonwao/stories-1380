# Eleven Floors

The regular monthly meeting of the Coquina Condominium Association, 400 South Lake Drive, Lantana, was called to order at 7:02 on a Thursday evening. Five board members present, quorum established. I have kept the minutes for nine years. Items one through six were the seawall assessment, the pool furniture bids, and the question of whether Mr. Levin's hibiscus had become, legally speaking, a hedge.

Item seven was a petition from Esther Fontaine, unit 1104, requesting permission to feed the cat behind the building. Where the form asked for the name of the animal, she had written: Cat.

The property manager from Sunstate read the file into the record. One cat, orange, male, left ear notched, first documented at the dumpster corral in 2019. Two citations issued to Mrs. Fontaine this year: one for feeding an animal in a common area, Rule 12.4(b), and one for an unapproved object in the eleventh-floor hallway. Fines accruing at twenty-five dollars a day.

Then the page the manager read more slowly. The cat was struck by a car on Ocean Avenue in May of last year. Hector from maintenance recovered the remains and buried them behind the pump room, the only soft ground on the property. The work order is in the file. It is logged under debris removal, because there is no other code.

Mr. Pruitt, treasurer, asked to confirm he understood the item. The animal is deceased. The resident is requesting permission to feed it. The manager confirmed both, and added that the food keeps disappearing.

Pruitt said the word for that is raccoons, and the word after raccoons is rabies, and the word after rabies is liability. He moved to deny. Seconded. Discussion opened.

Mrs. Okafor, vice president, asked Hector whether he had ever seen a raccoon at the bowl. Hector said no, ma'am. He said the bowl behind the building goes to the ants. The bowl that empties is the one on eleven.

At the manager's instruction, Hector had set a camera in the eleventh-floor hallway for fourteen nights. Exhibit C was entered into the record: forty seconds of an orange cat, left ear notched, eating from the bowl outside 1104 at 3:11 in the morning. The recording was triggered by a moth. The motion sensor does not register the cat. Sunstate's position is that the sensor is defective.

The service elevator runs to eleven most nights between 2:50 and 3:15. No fob is ever logged. Sunstate's position is that this is a fault.

Pruitt moved to replace the sensor. The motion died for lack of a second.

Mrs. Fontaine was recognized for three minutes of owner comment. She did not use them to argue that the cat was alive. She said she was standing next to Hector when he buried it. She held the box.

She said her husband couldn't come downstairs his last year. Dialysis on Mondays; by Tuesday he was strong enough to want company. Hector carried the cat up the service elevator every Tuesday for two years, and nobody reported it. "So fine me for that too," she said. Hector studied the carpet.

She said Saul did the naming in their family, and he never got around to this one. Saul kept every paper since 1979 in a drawer she still hasn't been able to thin out: warranties, permits, the variance for the hurricane shutters. She said she knows how this sounds. "It's eleven floors. He never once took the stairs while he was alive. I don't see why he'd start now." Nobody laughed, which was correct.

She said the board could approve it or deny it, but she wanted it in writing either way. Saul liked things in writing.

Pruitt withdrew his motion.

Mrs. Okafor moved to grant a variance to Rule 12.4(b): two bowls, one behind the building and one outside unit 1104, half a cup of dry food per night, for one cat, orange, deceased. Variance to renew annually. Pruitt, who has voted no on every expenditure since I have kept these minutes, moved to strike the renewal clause and let the variance run with the unit. Renewals invite challenges, he said, without looking up from his agenda.

Carried, five to zero. Pruitt asked that the record reflect his objection to the precedent and his vote in favor. The meeting adjourned at 8:51.

Mrs. Fontaine requested two certified copies. I walked them up myself the following week, because the mailroom loses things. The bowl was out beside her door, washed and full, and the variance was taped to the wall above it in a plastic sleeve, low, about a foot off the carpet.

That was a Wednesday morning. The bowl was empty.

Tuesdays were always his day.
